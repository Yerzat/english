Course Welcome

In week one we give an overview of planning and the approach taken in this course. We set AI planning in the context of task setting, plan execution and communication with people using plans. We list some of the very wide range of research areas being explored in the field of AI planning.

We also encourage you to start to use the course social platform including the discussion forums, the course wiki (which will continue to be built up beyond the end of the course as a continuing repository of useful information to assist you), share useful information via twitter with hash tag #aiplan, and to get a Second Life virtual world avatar and try the (experimental) virtual world meeting spaces to see if they are useful to course participants.

"Planning is the process of generating (possibly partial) representations of future behaviour prior to the use of such plans to constrain or control that behavior. The outcome is usually a set of actions, with temporal and other constraints on them, for execution by some agent or agents. As a core aspect of human intelligence, planning has been studied since the earliest days of AI and cognitive science. Planning research has led to many useful tools for real-world applications, and has yielded significant insights into the organization of behavior and the nature of reasoning about actions."

Austin Tate on 'Planning' in 'The MIT Encyclopedia of the Cognitive Sciences' (MITECS), 1999. Edited by Robert A. Wilson and Frank C. Keil. ISBN 0-262-73144-4. Page 652-3. http://ai.ato.ms/MITECS/Entry/tate.html
